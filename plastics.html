Microplastics

What are Microplastics?

Microplastics are extremely small pieces of plastic debris in the environment resulting from the disposal and breakdown of consumer products and industrial waste.

Formation of Microplastics

Microplastics are found everywhere. They are in oceans, lakes, soil, air, food, and our water. Sources of microplastics include landfills, personal items, factories and more. In less than 100 years we have produced 8 billion tons of plastic with only 10% of that being recycled. Microplastics form from the breaking down of materials into smaller particles.

Enviornmental Impact

  • Marine animals mistake the plastics for food
  • Act as rafts spreading invasive marine species
  • Marine animals risk ingesting toxic chemicals from the collection of pollutants on the surface of these microplastics
  • Humans consume more than 100,000 microplastic particles a year

How to Help

  • Reduce usage of single use products
  • Pick up litter
  • Recycle appropriately
  • Check products for microbeads
  • Educate others!
